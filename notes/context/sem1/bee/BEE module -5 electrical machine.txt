**Squirrel Cage Rotor**

A squirrel cage rotor consists of a cylindrical core with slots parallel to the shaft axis. The core is made up of a series of laminations that are stacked together to form a continuous cylinder. The slots are cut into the core to accommodate the conductors, which are typically made of copper or aluminum.

The squirrel cage rotor is designed to operate at high speeds, typically in the range of 1,000 to 10,000 rpm. It is commonly used in induction motors, which are widely used in industrial and commercial applications.

A slip ring rotor is similar to a squirrel cage rotor but has a few key differences. The core is made up of a series of laminations that are stacked together to form a continuous cylinder, but the slots are not cut into the core. Instead, the conductors are connected to the core through slip rings, which are small metal rings that rotate with the core.

The slip ring rotor is designed to operate at lower speeds than the squirrel cage rotor, typically in the range of 100 to 1,000 rpm. It is commonly used in synchronous motors, which are used in applications where high torque and low speed are required.
