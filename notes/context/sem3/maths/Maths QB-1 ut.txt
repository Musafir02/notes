**Continuous Random Variables**

A continuous random variable is a type of random variable that can take on any value within a given range or interval. This contrasts with discrete random variables, which can only take on specific, distinct values.

**Probability Density Functions (PDFs)**

A PDF is a mathematical function that describes the probability distribution of a continuous random variable. It is defined as a non-negative function f(x) that satisfies the following properties:

*   The total area under the curve of the PDF is equal to 1.
*   The probability that the random variable takes on a value within a given interval [a, b] is equal to the area under the curve between a and b.

The PDF of a continuous random variable is often denoted as f(x) and is used to calculate the probability of the random variable falling within a specific range.

P(1.5 < X < 1.8) = ∫[1.5, 1.8] f(x) dx
