1. Number/Letter/Symbol Series 
The goal is to identify the rule governing the sequence and use it to find the missing element. 
A. Number Series 
A Number Series is a sequence of numbers arranged in a specific, logical order based on a particular rule or 
pattern. Each number in the sequence is called a term. 
The primary objective of problems based on number series is to identify the underlying rule that governs the 
formation of the sequence. Once the rule is determined, it can be used to: 
• 
Find the next term(s) in the series. 
• 
Find a missing term within the series. 
• 
Sometimes, to identify the wrong term that does not follow the pattern. 
 
Strategy: Follow a step-by-step approach. 
1. Check for a common difference (subtraction). 
2. If not, check for a common ratio (division). 
3. Look for squares, cubes, or other powers. 
4. Check if it's a two-tier series (the pattern is in the differences between terms). 
5. Consider recursive patterns (each term depends on previous ones). 
6. Look for alternating patterns (two different rules for odd and even positions). 
Common Patterns with Examples: 
1. Arithmetic (Linear) Series: Constant difference between terms. 
• 
Example 1: 5, 8, 11, 14, 17, ? 
o +3 each time. Next term: 20 
• 
Example 2: 25, 23, 21, 19, 17, ? 
o -2 each time. Next term: 15 
2. Geometric (Exponential) Series: Constant ratio between terms. 
• 
Example 1: 2, 6, 18, 54, ? 
o ×3 each time. Next term: 162 
• 
Example 2: 128, 64, 32, 16, ? 
o ÷2 (or ×0.5) each time. Next term: 8 
3. Perfect Square/Cube Series: 
• 
Square Series: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, ? 
o 1², 2², 3², 4², 5²,... Next term: 36 (6²) 
• 
Cube Series: 1, 8, 27, 64, ? 
o 1³, 2³, 3³, 4³,... Next term: 125 (5³) 
4. Two-Tier Series: The difference between terms has its own pattern. 
• 
Example 1: 2, 4, 7, 11, 16, ? 
o Differences: +2, +3, +4, +5 
o Next difference: +6 

o Next term: 16 + 6 = 22 
• 
Example 2: 1, 2, 5, 10, 17, 26, ? 
o Differences: +1, +3, +5, +7, +9 (odd numbers) 
o Next difference: +11 
o Next term: 26 + 11 = 37 
5. Recursive Series: A term is based on one or more previous terms. 
• 
Fibonacci Type: Each term is the sum of the two previous terms. 
o Example: 3, 4, 7, 11, 18, 29, ? 
o 3+4=7, 4+7=11, 7+11=18, 11+18=29

Next term: (35 × 4) + 1 = 141

Odd Man Out  
 
Definition (in simple terms) 
Odd Man Out means: In a given sequence of numbers, letters, or symbols, all follow a rule/pattern except 
one. The task is to identify that one different (odd) element. 
Odd man out means all items follow the same rule except one, and you have to find the different one. 
1. Which number is the odd one out? 
2, 4, 6, 7, 8, 10 
Answer: 7 
• 
Why? 2, 4, 6, 8, and 10 are all even numbers. 7 is an odd number. 
 
2. Which number is the odd one out? 
3, 6, 9, 12, 14, 15 
Answer: 14 
• 
Why? 3, 6, 9, 12, and 15 are all multiples of 3. 14 is not (14 ÷ 3 = 4.666...). 
 

3. Which number is the odd one out? 
5, 10, 15, 20, 23, 25 
Answer: 23 
• 
Why? 5, 10, 15, 20, and 25 are all multiples of 5. 23 is not. 
 
4. Which number is the odd one out? 
11, 13, 17, 19, 21, 23 
Answer: 21 
• 
Why? 11, 13, 17, 19, and 23 are all prime numbers (only divisible by 1 and themselves). 21 is 
a composite number (3 x 7 = 21). 
 
5. Which number is the odd one out? 
16, 25, 36, 48, 49 
Answer: 48 
• 
Why? 16 (4x4), 25 (5x5), 36 (6x6), and 49 (7x7) are all perfect squares. 48 is not a perfect square. 
 
6. Which number is the odd one out? 
1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 30 
Answer: 30 
• 
Why? 1 (1x1), 4 (2x2), 9 (3x3), 16 (4x4), and 25 (5x5) are all perfect squares. 30 is not a perfect 
square. 
 
7. Which number is the odd one out? 
10, 20, 30, 35, 40, 50 
Answer: 35 
• 
Why? 10, 20, 30, 40, and 50 are all multiples of 10. 35 is a multiple of 5, but not 10.
